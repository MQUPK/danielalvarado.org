Selected works in philosophy of science, mathematical analysis, and computation.

**Mathematics as a Stance** 
Forthcoming paper arguing that mathematical necessity is epistemic rather than ontological, generated through recursive invariance under admissible transformations. Drawing on van Fraassen’s stance epistemology and Laudan’s reticulation model, the thesis develops a novel underdetermination result: any metaphysical program inferring ontology from mathematical structure is underdetermined by such structure.

**Harmonic Analysis & Causal Set Theory** 
Forthcoming paper developing summability methods at the interface of harmonic analysis and causal set theory, with applications to the Benincasa-Dowker-Glaser action.

**Modified Gram-Schmidt (MGS)** 
A double orthogonalization implementation yielding near-perfect reconstruction and numerical stability.

**Convex Optimization & Duality** 
Applications of probabilistic and deterministic gradient descent in optimization theory.
