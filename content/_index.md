I'm a senior at UC San Diego double-majoring in Philosophy and Mathematics, working at the intersection of philosophy of mathematics, philosophy of science, and foundations of physics.

My current research develops **Mathematical Epistemic Structuralism** (MES), a thesis arguing that mathematical necessity is epistemic rather than ontological, generated through recursive invariance under admissible transformations. The framework draws on van Fraassen's stance epistemology and Laudan's reticulation model, culminating in a novel underdetermination result: any metaphysical program inferring ontology from mathematical structure is underdetermined by that structure.

Complementary to MES, I am also researching methodology at the intersection of harmonic analysis and causal set theory, with applications to the Benincasa-Dowker-Glaser action.
